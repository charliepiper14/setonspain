Executive Summary: April 2026 Performance

The April Story in One Line

April was a softer month against an exceptionally strong March. We defended the High campaign abs top lead vs hihomes but the cushion compressed sharply, while lead volume dropped 30.1% on the HM Lead Form benchmark. The Medium campaign was the only positive volume mover.

April 2026 Wins

  • High Campaign Lead Defended: Held the abs top lead at 48.56% vs hihomes 47.41%, third consecutive month ahead of the primary competitor
  • Medium Campaign Lead Growth: 26.5 HM Lead Form submits vs March 24.5, the only campaign to grow leads month over month (+8.3%)
  • Account Mix Healthier: Medium share of total leads moved from 26.3% in March to 40.8% in April, reducing High campaign over-reliance
  • Structured Snippets Complete: All 57 remaining Medium ad groups completed, account-wide assets validation rolled in
  • Five New Ad Groups Live: 222 Tyrian, 223 Serenity Views, plus Eagle Hills, Amaris Villas and Palm Residences briefed
  • Bid Tweaks Productive: 4 of 7 target CPA increases delivered first conversions, Zinnia abs top up 17.3pp

April 2026 Challenges

  • HM Lead Form Volume Drop: 65.03 leads vs March 93.0, down 30.1%, driven entirely by High campaign
  • High Campaign Lead Erosion: 38.5 vs 68.5 leads, down 43.8%, CPA almost doubled from €62.56 to €123.39
  • Competitive Cushion Compressed: High abs top gap vs hihomes shrank from +9.92pp in March to +1.15pp in April. hihomes pushed +6.57pp on us in a single month
  • Medium Position Static: Despite the structured snippets and bid work, abs top gap vs hihomes widened slightly from -16.64pp to -17.64pp - rank lever is not bid-only
  • Market Softness: Tony confirmed wider Costa del Sol pullback, agents reporting longer decision cycles

High abs top fell to 42.95% as hihomes pushed bids up to 49.08%, swung 7.51pp in their favour. Spend down 12.6% week, GAds conv up 30%, blended CPA dropped sharply.

What This Means for May

March established a 9.92pp lead over hihomes on High abs top. April compressed that to 1.15pp - we are now in defence mode, not expansion. The strategy direction is right, the position is still ahead, but the cushion is gone. Holding the lead in May requires sustained pressure on High ad groups where the rank gap is widest. For Medium, the bid-only lever has run its course - the next move is landing page relevance and ad relevance score work alongside the structured snippets QS lift.

Funnel Labs and Website Decision

Locked decision date Wednesday 6 May 2026

Where We Are

Tony and Emma have been comparing Kenny's two routes (full WordPress rebuild at £8,500 plus £250/month maintenance, or CWV speed-only remediation) against a competing speed-only quote from another supplier at significantly lower cost. The decision was deferred from early April through to Wednesday 6 May to allow time for a side-by-side comparison and an internal Tony-Emma sit-down on additional website requirements.

Option A: Full Rebuild
  • £8,500 build cost, £250/month ongoing
  • Near-replica with CWV built in
  • Future-proof platform, no plugin compatibility issues
  • Long term option, addresses SiteGround question
  • Higher upfront, lower long term risk
Option B: Speed Only (Funnel Labs)
  • Approximately £5,500
  • Targets the 30 failing landing pages
  • Patches existing site, retains current architecture
  • Tony has indicated this is unlikely to be selected
Option C: Speed Only (Alternative)
  • Considerably lower than Funnel Labs quote
  • Same scope on paper, validation pending
  • Frees budget for sustained Google Ads investment
  • Short term fix, defers website rebuild question
Tony's Stated Position (April 22 Call)

"I'd rather spend it on Google Ads than a new website. But the problem is, if Google Ads are not converting, then that also makes it more difficult. So then we'll have to cut back on the spend on Google Ads to fund a new website."

The Commercial Tension

April spend ran at €240 per day on average, projecting to €7,200 monthly. Tony has flagged a possible €5,000 cap if April lead volume does not recover. The website decision has to be made against a backdrop of commercial discipline, not in isolation.

Heath Media Position

The website is converting and is not the immediate problem. CWV speed remediation would address the structural performance gap that affects quality score across the 30 failing pages. The choice between full rebuild and speed-only is fundamentally about timing and capital allocation, not about quality.

What Needs to Happen on 6 May

  • Tony and Emma confirm route (rebuild, Funnel Labs speed, alternative speed)
  • Confirm timing - immediate, deferred, or paused
  • Confirm Google Ads budget shape for May - hold, increase or pull back
  • Charles to update Kenny same day with the outcome
